My First Website

Date: 9 February 2026

Today I built my very first multi-page website using HTML5. I learned how to structure pages using semantic elements like <header>, <nav>, <main>, and <footer>, and how to link them all together with anchor tags.

The most exciting moment was clicking between pages for the first time and watching the navigation actually work. It felt like assembling a puzzle and seeing the full picture emerge. Web development and data analysis have more in common than I expected — both are about building structure from scattered parts.

#HTML #WebDevelopment #BeginnerCoder

Learning CSS Styling

Date: 10 February 2026

CSS transformed my plain HTML pages into something I am truly proud of. Today I worked with colours, fonts, margins, and the box model. I kept thinking about how similar this is to formatting a Power BI report — presentation matters just as much as the data behind it.

I applied a consistent colour scheme, added Google Fonts, and used Flexbox to centre elements cleanly. The most satisfying part was watching my navigation bar go from a stack of plain links to a sleek, styled menu.

#CSS #Styling #WebDesign

Photography Meets Data — My Favourite Crossover

Date: 11 February 2026

Today I had an interesting realisation while editing photos from a campus shoot. Photography and data analytics are more alike than most people think. Both require you to look beyond what is obvious — a good photographer sees light and composition where others see a plain scene, and a good analyst sees patterns and outliers where others see a plain spreadsheet.

Framing a shot is like building a dashboard: you decide what to include, what to cut, and how to guide the viewer's eye to what matters. I am grateful that my studies at BAC are teaching me both — the technical and the creative.

#Photography #DataAnalytics #BIDA
